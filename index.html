About

I am a postgraduate in Physics from NIT Durgapur, specializing in deep learning for scientific and biomedical imaging. My work bridges AI, materials science, and healthcare, focusing on segmentation, generative modeling, and interpretability of microscopy and medical images.

My master’s thesis introduced a three-phase deep learning pipeline using Attention U-Net and CycleGAN to address nanoparticle segmentation under data scarcity, resulting in a conference paper (COMSYS 2025) and an open-source release: F-ANcGAN.

Currently, I am a Research Intern at the Indian Statistical Institute (ISI) Kolkata, developing deep learning models for prostate cancer grading and exploring multimodal learning and uncertainty estimation in pathology. My broader interests span generative AI, diffusion models, and LLMs for healthcare.

Research

F-ANcGAN: Attention-Enhanced CycleGAN for Nanoparticle Image Generation

Developed a three-phase segmentation–generation pipeline combining Attention U-Net and CycleGAN to improve nanoparticle segmentation under data scarcity. Published in COMSYS 2025.

Projects

Multimodal Diffusion for Biomedical Imaging

Exploring multimodal conditioning with text and image embeddings for diffusion-based segmentation under domain shift in medical datasets.

GAN-driven Synthetic Data Generation

Developing attention-guided GANs to synthesize microscopy images under low-data regimes, enabling data-efficient biomedical segmentation.

LLMs for Scientific Reasoning

Investigating the integration of language models with scientific imaging tasks for explainability and hypothesis generation in AI-driven research.
